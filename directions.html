Finding your way

中文翻譯 (Chinese Translation)

Getting to us is relatively straightforward. Hickory is uniquely located on Penang Hill's eastern slope, overlooking bustling Georgetown.

Here, you will find directions to get to us. Enjoy the journey to Hickory and we look forward to receiving you!


Directions


1. By Train

Make your way to Penang Hill Railway Lower Station. Information about tickets and train times can be found here.

The train ride is an experience in itself. The Swiss-engineered Funicular train travels each way on a regular basis. The 2 cars (at opposite ends of the track) are joined by a cable which goes over a pulleyed mechanism at Upper Station. Both cars move simultaneously on a single track which shunts mid-way to allow them to pass. The only other such rail system in Asia is on Hong Kong's Victoria Peak.

If you are coming by car you can park at Penang Hill Parking before purchasing your ticket.

Alternatively, there is an open car park nearby.

Take the train and alight at Lower Tunnel Station (stop "3" in the map below). The stopping train service is every half-hour (hourly on weekends). Board the front cabin and inform the driver that you wish to alight at "Lower Tunnel Station" as the train does not stop there automatically. Retain your ticket for the return journey.

Alight at Lower Tunnel Station, walk for 5 minutes along Lower Tunnel "Road" and you will reach Hickory (Bungalow "14" ). Hickory is signposted.


Alternative train stop:

You can also stop at Upper Station (stop "1" ). A 15-minute stroll down by-paths A & F leads to Hickory.

2. Hiking

You can also hike up to Hickory.

The hiking trail starts near Lower Station and ends at Top Station. The start point of the trail is here.

The trail shadows the railway track initially and brushes past Hickory near the top. The hike takes around 90 minutes.


3. By Jeep

Hickory is also accessible by 4-wheel drive vehicles via the steep and winding Jeep Track that starts at the Botanical Gardens. This access is restricted to residents and staff associated with Penang Hill.

Exceptionally, arrangements can be made to ferry guests to Hickory via this route provided advance notice is given. However, the train is a faster and more convenient option.

Attractions on Penang Hill

中文翻譯 (Chinese Translation)

The cool climate, high elevation and seclusion of Penang Hill makes it a prime location for some excellent attractions.


David Brown’s

Strawberry Hill is on a hillock immediately above Upper Station. Its first owner, David Brown, grew strawberries there.

David Brown was a contemporary of Captain Francis Light. On his estates he grew nutmeg, cloves & pepper. He also built Brown House and Mon Craig.

David Brown's Cottage was rebuilt in 2005 to capture its former glory. It now houses an upmarket establishment called David Brown's Restaurant & Tea House. It's a place for English tea, scones, strawberry-and-cream; and where dressing up for dinner is de rigeuer. Its menu is steep with nostalgia for the colonial era.


Bellvue

Bellevue Hotel was originally the private residence of William Halliburton, the first Sheriff of Prince of Wales Island (Penang), under the British East India Company, in the early 19th century. The hotel also runs an aviary showcasing a selection of birds.


Monkey Cup Garden

The cool climate of Penang Hill is ideal for setting up this exotic garden. The Monkey Cup Garden attempts to faithfully recreate the habitat of pitcher plants, together with other plants found in the pitcher plant ecology such as ferns, orchids and shrubs. They are carnivorous plants with mechanisms to trap prey, mostly insects. The pitcher plant is often called monkey cup as monkeys are fond of drinking from the pitchers.

Apart from the Nepenthes, other carnivorous plants are also grown here including the Venus Flytrap and the Sarracenia. Enthusiasts can also purchase pitcher plants at the garden shop. Books and other material related to pitcher plants are also on sale.

The garden is located about 1.5 km from the Penang Hill Police Station and is signposted. To reach the garden, take the path off Jalan Tuanku Yahya Petra in front of Top Station.

Who we are

We are the 5th generation descendents of arrivals to Penang from Ceylon (now Sri Lanka) in the late 19th Century, a part of a larger influx who arrived at the behest of the British colonial administration to assist in the development and administration of the land.

Some worked for the Government of the day. Some were involved in the development of the country's infrastructure (transport, health, education). Still others opened up the hinterland for growing rubber, a commodity in great demand then. Our origins were in plantations and education. With each successive generation the family gradually dispersed far and wide across the Commonwealth.

At Family gatherings, conversation usually gravitates towards the days of the earlier generations, and their lives and their achievements in Penang.

I am Raj, the current owner of Hickory, and a member of the 4th generation. Through a series of fortunate events, Hickory has been placed under my care. I have decided to renovate and convert Hickory into a bed and breakfast to preserve the historical significance of the building and of the lineage that has led to this moment.

An excerpt from the book The Penang Story touches on the activities of 2 such individuals of the 1st and 2nd generation arrivals (my great-grandfather and my grandfather, respectively):

      "...Other early arrivals were Mr. 'Brown' C.Cathiravelu, a well-known Ceylon Tamil entrepreneur who bought over Penang's famous E&O Hotel just to prove a point and his son-in-law, Mr. 'Tanjong Bungah' K.Sinnatamby who was the owner of 100 acres of land in Tanjong Bungah which he later developed..."

Hickory replicates the atmosphere of a bygone era where the pace was slower and life more tranquil. It is also a focus for the diaspora to call Home.

We hope to provide You with an experience far removed from the hustle and bustle of City life; a back-to-nature experience with long walks in the woods; and an atmosphere that encourages reading and writing, in a carefree, cooler and meditative setting.

We invite you to Hickory to savour the solitude of nightfall and the splendour of daybreak on Penang Hill.
